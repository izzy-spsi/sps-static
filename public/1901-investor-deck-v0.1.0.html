The Supermarket Variance
A supermarket is NOT a permitted use on Emmons Avenue zoning. 1901 holds the only existing variance allowing it. This use right does not transfer to any neighboring property — it is exclusive to this lot and cannot be replicated.
National grocery interest for the ground floor creates a potential long-term NNN anchor lease — the highest-credit, lowest-management tenancy available in retail real estate.

04
Location Analysis
Sheepshead Bay,
Brooklyn
Sheepshead Bay is one of Brooklyn's most distinctive waterfront neighborhoods — a dense, established community centered on the Emmons Avenue promenade, known for its fishing boats, restaurants, and the character of a working waterfront town inside New York City.
The Emmons Avenue corridor has seen increasing pressure from new residential development — The Vue directly west of 1901 is the most recent proof point that condo buyers and renters are willing to pay a premium for the waterfront address. 1901 is the largest remaining underdeveloped site on the corridor.

Special Sheepshead Bay District
The SB overlay (Special Sheepshead Bay District) governs streetscape, sidewalk extensions, and building height/setback. The proposed C4-5X rezoning works within this framework — and the 1901 site is in Area B, which has specific provisions favorable to the proposed development.

Existing Asset Bridge
While entitlements are underway, the existing building continues operating. The current tenant base (supermarket, Patrizia's, café, offices) provides income during the approval period — reducing carrying cost and demonstrating continued commercial viability to lenders.
Pro forma Scenario 2 (NNN renegotiated leases at 95% market) can be implemented immediately post-acquisition — establishing institutional-quality income before construction begins.
ULURP Precedent
C4-5X rezonings with affordable housing commitments (25%) have strong approval records in Brooklyn's outer borough waterfront corridors. The 25% affordable component directly addresses CB15's stated housing priorities.
