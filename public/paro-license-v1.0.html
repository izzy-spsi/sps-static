Section 01
Parties

1.1Licensor: Evithé Biotechnology Ltd., a company incorporated in New Zealand ("Licensor" or "Evithé").

1.2Licensee: Paro Wellness Inc., a Delaware corporation to be formed ("Licensee" or "Paro"). Until formal incorporation, Paro is operating under incubation by Super Power Studios Inc. ("SPS").

1.3Effective Date: TBD — upon execution by both parties.

⚠ Recusal Protocol

John Clough serves as a Director of Evithé Biotechnology and as an advisor to Paro Wellness Inc. In accordance with standard corporate governance and conflict-of-interest protocols, John Clough shall recuse himself from negotiations on behalf of both parties. All material terms shall be negotiated at arm's length by parties without conflict of interest. This recusal shall be documented and acknowledged by both Licensor and Licensee prior to execution.

1.4Recital: Licensor possesses certain proprietary rights, including patents, trade secrets, and clinical data, relating to a hormesis-derived ginger extract ("Licensed Extract"). Licensee desires to obtain an exclusive license to use the Licensed Extract in consumer wellness products in the Territory. Licensor is willing to grant such license on the terms and conditions set forth herein.

Section 02
Grant of License

2.1Exclusive License Grant. Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Licensor hereby grants to Licensee an exclusive, non-transferable license to:

(a) Use the Licensed Extract (Evithé's hormesis-derived ginger extract, standardized to approximately 20% gingerols/shogaols) in the formulation of consumer wellness products;

(b) Market, sell, and distribute consumer wellness products containing the Licensed Extract within the Territory;

(c) Reference and use the Phase 1 clinical trial data (50 participants, 7 validated inflammatory biomarkers, 0 adverse events) in marketing materials, product packaging, investor communications, and regulatory filings, subject to applicable law and the guidelines set forth in Exhibit C.

2.2Territory. The license granted herein applies exclusively to the United States of America. Licensee shall have a Right of First Negotiation to expand the Territory to Canada and Mexico for a period of TBD days following written notice by Licensee of its intent to expand.

2.3Exclusivity Period. The exclusive nature of the license shall remain in effect for the Initial Term, provided Licensee meets the Performance Minimums set forth in Section 4.4. Proposed initial exclusivity: 3 years with renewal options subject to performance.

2.4Sublicensing. Licensee shall not sublicense any rights granted under this Agreement without the prior written consent of Licensor, which shall not be unreasonably withheld.

Note: The exclusivity granted herein is limited to consumer wellness product categories. Licensor retains all rights to the Licensed Extract for pharmaceutical drug development, clinical research, and non-consumer commercial applications.
Section 03
Licensed Intellectual Property

3.1The Licensed Extract. Licensor's proprietary hormesis-derived ginger extract, manufactured using Licensor's patented process, standardized to approximately 20% gingerols/shogaols, delivering 100mg active phenolics per recommended serving, as further described in Exhibit A.

3.2Clinical Data Rights. All Phase 1 clinical trial data, biomarker results, safety data, and efficacy data generated by or on behalf of Licensor, as further described in Exhibit C. Licensee's right to reference clinical data is limited to accurate, non-misleading summaries consistent with applicable FTC and FDA guidelines.

3.3Patents. Licensor's patents relating to: (a) the hormesis extraction process, and (b) the composition of the Licensed Extract. Patent numbers: To be listed upon execution — 3 patents on file with Evithé Biotechnology.

3.4New Clinical Data. As new clinical data relating to the Licensed Extract is generated during the Term, Licensee shall have the right to reference and use such data in marketing, claims, and communications upon providing 30 days written notice to Licensor. Licensor shall cooperate in providing documentation reasonably necessary to support Licensee's claims.

3.5Trademark. This Agreement does not grant Licensee any rights to use Licensor's trade name "Evithé" or any associated trademarks in consumer-facing marketing without separate written authorization. Licensee may reference the Evithé partnership in investor and B2B contexts.

Section 04
Financial Terms

4.1Royalty Rate. Licensee shall pay Licensor a royalty on Net Revenue from Licensed Products at the following tiered rates:

Tier Net Revenue Royalty Rate
Tier 1 $0 – $1,000,000 7%
Tier 2 $1,000,001 – $5,000,000 6%
Tier 3 $5,000,001+ 5%

For purposes hereof, "Net Revenue" means gross revenue from Licensed Products less returns, chargebacks, and sales taxes, but not including royalty payments owed hereunder.

4.2Royalty Reporting. Licensee shall provide Licensor with monthly royalty reports within 30 days of the close of each calendar month, detailing unit sales, gross revenue, deductions, and net royalty owed, accompanied by payment.

Section 05
Supply Agreement

5.1Guaranteed Supply. Licensor shall guarantee supply of the Licensed Extract sufficient to meet Licensee's reasonable production requirements, subject to advance purchase orders and lead times set forth herein.

5.2Extract Pricing. TBD — Proposed: fixed price per gram with annual CPI (US Urban) adjustment cap of 3% per year. Pricing to be memorialized in Exhibit B.

5.3Quality Specifications. Each batch of Licensed Extract delivered to Licensee shall:

(a) Meet the specifications set forth in Exhibit A, including minimum 20% gingerols/shogaols standardization;

(b) Be accompanied by a Certificate of Analysis (COA) from an independent, accredited laboratory;

(c) Meet all applicable FDA dietary supplement quality standards (21 CFR Part 111).

5.4Lead Time. Proposed: 90-day advance purchase orders. Parties shall agree on a rolling forecast mechanism to reduce supply risk as Licensee scales.

5.5Supply Continuity. In the event Licensor reasonably anticipates an inability to fulfill purchase orders, Licensor shall provide Licensee with no less than 6 months advance written notice and shall use commercially reasonable efforts to assist Licensee in qualifying an alternative approved source for the Licensed Extract during any supply disruption period.

5.6GMP Compliance. The Licensed Extract shall be manufactured in GMP-compliant facilities meeting FDA 21 CFR Part 111 requirements or internationally equivalent standards acceptable to Licensee.

Section 06
Regulatory

6.1NDI Notification. Licensee shall be solely responsible for filing an FDA New Dietary Ingredient (NDI) notification for the Licensed Extract prior to commercial sale in the United States, at Licensee's cost. Licensor shall cooperate fully in providing the documentation, safety data, and technical information required to support the NDI filing.

6.2Regulatory Cooperation. Licensor shall cooperate in providing documentation reasonably required for Licensee's regulatory filings, certifications (including NSF), and retail compliance programs, at no additional cost to Licensee except for extraordinary out-of-pocket expenses approved in advance.

6.3Marketing Claims. Licensee is solely responsible for ensuring all marketing, advertising, and labeling claims comply with applicable FTC and FDA regulations. Licensee shall not make any claims that constitute drug claims under 21 USC § 321(g). Licensor shall review and approve any direct references to Evithé's clinical trial data in consumer-facing materials prior to publication, such approval not to be unreasonably withheld or delayed beyond 10 business days.

6.4Adverse Event Reporting. Each party shall promptly notify the other of any adverse events associated with use of the Licensed Extract that come to its attention.

Section 07
Confidentiality

7.1Mutual Non-Disclosure. Each party agrees to hold the terms and conditions of this Agreement in strict confidence and not to disclose them to any third party without the prior written consent of the other party, except as required by applicable law or as otherwise provided herein.

7.2Investor Disclosure. Licensee may disclose the existence and a reasonable summary of this Agreement to potential investors, lenders, and financial advisors under a confidentiality agreement providing no less protection than this Section 7. Full terms of this Agreement shall not be disclosed without Licensor's written consent, except as required by applicable securities law.

7.3Permitted Disclosures. Each party may disclose Confidential Information to its directors, officers, employees, advisors, attorneys, and accountants who have a need to know and who are bound by obligations of confidentiality no less restrictive than this Section.

7.4Duration. Confidentiality obligations shall survive termination of this Agreement for a period of 5 years.

Section 08
Term & Termination

8.1Initial Term. Proposed: 5 years from the Effective Date.

8.2Renewal. Proposed: Two (2) renewal options of two (2) years each, exercisable by Licensee upon 90 days written notice prior to expiration of the then-current Term, subject to mutual agreement on updated Performance Minimums.

8.3Termination for Cause. Either party may terminate this Agreement upon a material breach by the other party that remains uncured 60 days after written notice specifying the breach in reasonable detail.

8.4Termination for Non-Performance. Licensor may, at its option, convert the license grant from exclusive to non-exclusive (but not terminate the Agreement in its entirety) upon Licensee's failure to achieve Performance Minimums in accordance with Section 4.4.

8.5Effect of Termination. Upon termination: (a) all licenses granted hereunder shall immediately terminate; (b) Licensee shall cease all use of Licensed IP; (c) outstanding royalties shall be paid within 30 days; (d) each party shall return or destroy Confidential Information of the other party.

(a) Licensor is the sole owner of the Licensed IP and has the full right and authority to grant the licenses set forth herein;

(b) To Licensor's knowledge, the Licensed IP does not infringe any third-party intellectual property rights;

(d) There are no pending or threatened claims against the Licensed IP known to Licensor;

9.2Licensee Represents and Warrants:

(b) Licensee shall comply with all applicable federal, state, and local laws in connection with the marketing, sale, and distribution of Licensed Products;

10.2Dispute Resolution. Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be resolved by binding arbitration administered by JAMS pursuant to its Commercial Arbitration Rules. The arbitration shall be conducted in [City, State — TBD] before a single arbitrator. Judgment on the award may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

10.3Equitable Relief. Notwithstanding Section 10.2, either party may seek emergency equitable relief before a court of competent jurisdiction to prevent irreparable harm pending the outcome of arbitration, particularly in matters involving unauthorized use of Licensed IP or breach of confidentiality obligations.

10.4Attorneys' Fees. In any dispute arising under this Agreement, the prevailing party shall be entitled to recover its reasonable attorneys' fees and costs.
