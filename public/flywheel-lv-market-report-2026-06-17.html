MEETING TALKING POINTS
01

Supply is not the problem

Las Vegas has the largest driver pool in the network at 586/day. Uber is sending 60,950 offers/week. The pipeline is full. The problem is what happens after the offer.

02

Drivers see offers — and ignore them

49% response rate but only 4% acceptance rate. Drivers are actively seeing these offers and choosing not to act. This is a behavioral and incentive problem — not a technology or visibility problem.

03

The conversion math is broken

It takes 39.63 offers to generate 1 completed trip in Las Vegas. The network average is dramatically lower (Palm Springs: 6.08, FTL: 13.56). Every wasted offer is lost revenue for drivers and Uber.

04

Demand is recovering but still down 24% from peak

After a sharp MoM -45% drop, payouts have bounced back 2 consecutive weeks (+10% then +27%). Current week $23.6K vs peak $31K. The trend is positive but fragile.

05

Concentration risk in top 4 fleets

Yellow Cab, NewCab 21, Checker Cab, and Star Cab = 71% of all revenue. Bottom 3 fleets (Desert Cab, Deluxe Taxi, A Cab) averaged under $300/week combined. Engagement is uneven.

06

Flywheel + Kaptyn opportunity

The data shows a clear lever: improve acceptance and completion rates. Even moving acceptance from 4% → 10% would roughly 2.5× completed trips — without adding a single driver. That's the value proposition.
