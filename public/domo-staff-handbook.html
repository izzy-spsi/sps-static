Part Two
Your Employment

At-Will Employment

Important — Please Read

Your employment with Domo Cafe is at-will. This means either you or Domo Cafe may end the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause, and with or without advance notice, subject to applicable law.

This handbook is not a contract of employment and does not guarantee employment for any specific duration. Nothing in this handbook — including confirmation as a permanent team member — alters your at-will status or creates any implied employment contract.

No manager, supervisor, or representative of Domo Cafe has the authority to modify your at-will status except the Owner, in a written agreement signed by both parties.

60-Day Probation & Permanent Team Member Status

  • All new staff begin on a 60-day probationary period from their first day of training
  • Performance, attendance, and conduct are reviewed throughout the period
  • Staff who meet expectations are confirmed as permanent team members at the end of the period
What does it mean to be a permanent team member?

Being confirmed as a permanent team member means you have completed your 60-day probationary period and have demonstrated the conduct, reliability, and performance Domo Cafe expects. It signals a mutual commitment — Domo Cafe is invested in your growth, and you are a core part of this team.

Being confirmed as permanent does not change your scheduled hours (hours are determined by operational needs), does not constitute a contract of employment, and does not alter your at-will status. It means you are no longer on probationary status and are considered a regular ongoing employee.

Background Check Notice

As a condition of employment, all team members may be subject to a background check conducted at some point during their 90-day probation period. A separate written disclosure and consent form will be provided to you before any background check is conducted. Background check results that reveal disqualifying information may result in the termination of employment, consistent with applicable law.

Food Handler Certification

⚠ Deadline: June 4, 2026 — All staff must hold a valid Food Handler’s Card by end of training week.
  • Domo Cafe will provide access to an approved course for any staff member without a valid card
  • If your existing card expires within 3 months — renew before your first day of service

Sexual Harassment Prevention Training

California law requires all employees to complete sexual harassment prevention training. Domo Cafe will provide and schedule this training for all team members.

  • All employees: 1 hour of training, to be completed within 14 days of your first day of employment, and every 2 years thereafter
  • Supervisors and managers: 2 hours of training, to be completed within 14 days of hire or promotion, and every 2 years thereafter
  • Training covers: types of harassment, applicable laws, how to report, and corrective procedures
  • Completion records are maintained on file. You will be notified when training is scheduled.
Part Three
Your Rights & Benefits

Pay & Compensation Overview

Your hourly rate and pay schedule are detailed in Exhibit A attached to this handbook. Your pay is issued bi-weekly. Pay periods run Monday through Sunday for two consecutive weeks. Payday is the Friday following the close of each two-week pay period. If payday falls on a holiday, payment is issued the preceding business day.

California law does not permit tips to be credited toward your base hourly wage. Your base pay rate applies regardless of tips received.

Workweek: Domo Cafe’s workweek runs from Monday through Sunday. This fixed workweek is used to calculate overtime and scheduling across all roles.

Overtime

California Daily Overtime — Your Rights

California overtime law is stricter than federal law. Overtime is calculated daily, not just weekly.

  • Hours 8–12 in a single workday: 1.5× your regular rate
  • Hours over 12 in a single workday: 2× your regular rate
  • First 8 hours on the 7th consecutive day in a workweek: 1.5× your regular rate
  • Hours over 8 on the 7th consecutive day: 2× your regular rate
  • Hours over 40 in a workweek (not covered above): 1.5× your regular rate

Overtime must be approved in advance by your manager. Working unauthorized overtime may result in disciplinary action, but you will always be compensated for all hours worked.

Meal & Rest Breaks

California Break Rights — Mandatory

These are legal rights, not optional perks. You are entitled to breaks as follows:

  • Meal Break: One 30-minute unpaid meal break for any shift over 5 hours, scheduled near the midpoint of your shift. A second 30-minute meal break is required for shifts over 10 hours.
  • Rest Break: One 10-minute paid rest break for every 4 hours worked (or a major fraction thereof — more than 2 hours). A standard 8-hour shift includes 2 rest breaks.
  • Waiver: A meal break may be waived only if your shift is 6 hours or less and both you and your manager agree in writing.
If your break is missed, late, or cut short: You are entitled to 1 additional hour of pay at your regular rate for each violation. Never skip a break without reporting it. Report any missed or shortened break to your manager immediately — or to the Owner if your manager was the cause.
  • Paid rest breaks cannot be skipped to leave early or arrive late
  • You may not be required to remain on premises during an off-duty meal break
  • Your manager will schedule breaks. If a break is delayed significantly, notify your manager.

Paid Sick Leave

California Healthy Workplaces Healthy Families Act

All employees — full-time, part-time, and seasonal — are entitled to paid sick leave under California law.

  • Accrual: 1 hour of paid sick leave for every 30 hours worked
  • Cap: Accrual may be capped at 80 hours; use is capped at 40 hours (5 days) per year
  • When you can use it: Your own illness, injury, or preventive care appointment; care for a family member’s illness or appointment; issues related to domestic violence, sexual assault, or stalking
  • Your balance will appear on your pay stub each pay period
Retaliation prohibited: You cannot be disciplined, terminated, or penalized in any way for using your accrued sick leave. An absence covered by sick leave does not count as an attendance violation under our arrival policy.

Family & Medical Leave (CFRA / PDL)

California Family Rights Act (CFRA)

After 12 months of employment and 1,250 hours worked in the prior 12 months, you are eligible for up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave per year for:

  • Birth, adoption, or foster placement of a child (baby bonding)
  • Your own serious health condition
  • Care for a seriously ill family member (child, parent, spouse, domestic partner, grandparent, grandchild, or sibling)
Pregnancy Disability Leave (PDL)

Regardless of length of employment, if you are disabled due to pregnancy, childbirth, or a related medical condition, you may be entitled to up to 4 months of unpaid, job-protected leave. This is separate from and in addition to CFRA leave.

To request any family or medical leave, contact Izzy Aala, Owner, as early as possible. Leave requests will be handled confidentially.

Lactation Accommodation

California law requires Domo Cafe to provide reasonable break time and a private, clean space — not a restroom — for nursing employees to express breast milk during the workday. If you need lactation accommodation, speak with the Owner directly. Your request will be handled confidentially.

Voting & Jury Duty Leave

Voting Leave

If you do not have sufficient time outside of your working hours to vote in a statewide election, you are entitled to up to 2 hours of paid time off to vote. Advance notice of at least 2 days before the election is required. Voting leave will not be approved without prior notification. Voting leave should be taken at the beginning or end of your shift when possible.

Jury Duty

If you receive a jury summons, notify your manager as soon as possible. You will not be retaliated against or penalized for serving on jury duty. California law requires us to provide time off for jury service.

Reporting Time Pay & Split Shift Premium

Reporting Time Pay

If you report to work as scheduled and are sent home early — or are not given work — you are entitled to pay for at least half of your scheduled shift, with a minimum of 2 hours and a maximum of 4 hours pay. This applies regardless of the reason, including slow business or overstaffing. If you are sent home early, confirm with your manager that reporting time pay will be included in your paycheck.

Split Shift Premium

If your shift is split with an unpaid gap of more than 1 hour (outside of a standard meal break), you may be entitled to a split shift premium equal to 1 additional hour at the state minimum wage — unless your total compensation already exceeds the minimum wage by that amount. If you work a split shift, confirm with your manager that the premium is applied.

Final Paycheck

When You Will Receive Your Final Pay
  • Terminated (involuntary): Final paycheck due immediately on your last day
  • Resigned without notice: Final paycheck due within 72 hours of your last day
  • Resigned with 72+ hours notice: Final paycheck due on your last day of work

California law imposes waiting time penalties of one additional day’s wages for each day a final paycheck is late, up to 30 days. If you believe your final paycheck is late or incorrect, contact Izzy Aala, Owner, immediately.

Pay Stubs

Your pay stub is issued every pay period and will include the following information as required by California Labor Code §226:

  • Gross wages earned for the pay period
  • Total hours worked (all non-exempt employees)
  • Itemized deductions
  • Net wages
  • Dates of the pay period
  • Your name and last four digits of your Social Security Number
  • Domo Cafe’s legal name and business address
  • Your applicable hourly rate(s) and hours worked at each rate
  • Accrued paid sick leave balance

If you believe there is an error in your pay stub or paycheck, report it to your manager immediately. Do not wait — errors must be corrected promptly.

Workers’ Compensation

Domo Cafe carries workers’ compensation insurance as required by California law. If you are injured on the job — no matter how minor — report it to your manager immediately. Do not delay. Delayed reporting can affect your ability to receive benefits.

  • Workers’ compensation covers medical treatment and wage replacement for work-related injuries and illnesses
  • You will not be retaliated against for filing a workers’ compensation claim
  • A notice of workers’ compensation rights is posted in the workplace

Standards & Conduct

Hair

  • FOH: Long hair must be tied back or secured during service
  • BOH: Hairnet and hat required at all times in the kitchen — no exceptions
  • All hair colors welcome. Hair must be fully secured before entering any food prep or service area.

Nails

Fragrance

Personal Hygiene

Team members are expected to report to every shift clean and well-groomed. This includes a freshly laundered uniform, clean hair, and attention to personal hygiene. Strong body odor — whether from insufficient hygiene or overpowering fragrance — is not appropriate in a food service environment where guests and coworkers are in close proximity. We ask that all team members be considerate of guests and coworkers in this regard.

Tattoos

  • ✓  Visible tattoos welcome
  • ✗  Tattoos with profanity or explicit imagery must be covered during service

Food Safety & Hygiene

Handwashing (required):

  • Before starting your shift and after any break
  • After handling raw proteins, trash, or chemicals
  • After touching your face, hair, or phone
  • After using the restroom — without exception
  • Soap and warm water, 20 seconds minimum. Gloves do not replace handwashing.

Eating & Drinking

  • No eating or drinking on the service floor or in food prep areas
  • Staff meals and beverages in the designated break area only

Illness Policy — Do NOT report to work if you are experiencing:

  • Vomiting, diarrhea, or nausea
  • Fever of 100°F or higher
  • Jaundice, sore throat with fever, or infected skin lesions
  • Notify your shift lead as early as possible — do not wait until your shift starts
  • Absences for illness may be covered by your accrued paid sick leave (see Part Three)
  • If you call out sick and have a scheduled shift the following day, management will work to reassign that shift to another available team member. Notify management as early as possible to allow adequate time to arrange coverage.

Phone Policy

  • Personal phones kept out of sight during your shift
  • No phone use on the service floor or behind the counter while guests are present
  • Urgent calls: step to the designated staff area and keep it brief
  • Phones permitted during scheduled breaks only

Arrival & Attendance

  • Arrive on time and ready — in uniform, hair secured, before your shift starts
  • 5 or more minutes late without prior notice = documented late arrival
  • 3 documented late arrivals = corrective action review
  • If you know you will be late or absent: contact your shift lead or manager immediately — do not wait until your start time
  • Absences covered by accrued sick leave will not count as attendance violations (see Paid Sick Leave)

Social Media Policy

You are free to use social media on your own time. When you do, please keep the following in mind:

  • Do not photograph, film, or share images of guests without their explicit consent
  • Do not post confidential business information — including recipes, financials, internal processes, or guest information
  • Do not post content that could harm, embarrass, or misrepresent the Domo Cafe brand
  • Be thoughtful about how you represent yourself as a team member in public forums
Protected Activity — Federal Law

Nothing in this policy restricts your right to discuss your wages, hours, working conditions, or other terms of employment with your coworkers or in any public forum. This is protected activity under the National Labor Relations Act (NLRA) and cannot be prohibited or penalized by your employer.

Post-Employment

The following obligations survive the end of your employment with Domo Cafe:

  • Confidentiality: You may not share trade secrets, recipes, financial information, supplier details, guest data, or any proprietary operational information at any time — including after your employment ends
  • Non-disparagement of false statements: You may not make false or defamatory statements about Domo Cafe, its team members, or its guests after your employment ends
Your Rights Are Protected

Nothing in this post-employment policy restricts your right to share honest opinions about your work experience. Only false or defamatory statements and disclosure of confidential business information are restricted after employment ends.

Clock-In & Clock-Out Policy

You are responsible for accurately clocking in and out for every shift using Toast. This is not optional — your clock records directly affect your pay and your teammates’ tip calculations. Because tips are distributed proportionally by hours worked per shift, inaccurate time entries affect everyone on the floor, not just you.

The Rules

  • Clock in when your shift begins — not before, not after
  • Clock out immediately when your shift ends
  • If you take a meal break off-premises, clock out and back in
  • Never clock in or out for another team member — this is a Zero Tolerance offense (falsifying records)

Forgot to Clock Out?

If you forget to clock out, your hours will be reverted to your scheduled shift for that day. You will not be paid beyond your scheduled end time for a missed clock-out. A formal reminder will be issued and documented. Repeat occurrences are subject to corrective action under our progressive discipline process.

Disputes

If you believe your recorded hours are incorrect, notify your manager before the end of your shift whenever possible — or no later than the following business day. Time record disputes cannot be accepted more than one pay period after the shift in question.

Smoke-Free & Vape-Free Policy

Domo Cafe is a completely smoke-free and vape-free environment. The following are strictly prohibited:

  • Smoking, vaping, or use of e-cigarettes, tobacco, or any nicotine products anywhere on Domo Cafe premises
  • Smoking, vaping, or use of any such products while wearing the Domo Cafe uniform — whether on or off premises. Stepping outside to take out trash or perform any other work duty does not create an exception.
If you need to smoke or vape, you must be off the clock, off premises, and out of uniform. There are no exceptions to this policy while in uniform.

Harassment & Discrimination — How to Report

If you experience or witness harassment, discrimination, or any other misconduct — whether from a coworker, manager, or guest — you are encouraged and expected to report it. You will not face retaliation for making a good-faith complaint.

How to Report

  • Step 1: Report to the Owner — in person, by phone, or in writing
  • Step 2: If the concern involves the Owner directly, or if you are not comfortable reporting internally, you may contact the California Civil Rights Department (CRD) at calcivilrights.ca.gov or (800) 884-1684

What Happens After You Report

  • All complaints will be investigated promptly, fairly, and as confidentially as possible
  • You will be kept informed of the status and outcome to the extent permitted by law and confidentiality obligations
  • If the investigation finds a violation occurred, appropriate corrective action will be taken — up to and including immediate termination
  • Retaliation of any kind against any person who reports in good faith is a zero tolerance violation

Workplace Relationship Disclosure

Team members who enter into a romantic or personal relationship with another Domo Cafe team member are required to notify management promptly. This allows management to assess any potential conflicts of interest — particularly where one person may have scheduling, supervisory, or performance influence over the other.

  • Disclosure must be made to the Owner or a manager as soon as the relationship begins
  • Disclosure is not grounds for disciplinary action
  • Management may make scheduling adjustments to address any conflict of interest
  • Failure to disclose a relationship that creates a conflict of interest, or any conduct that disrupts the workplace or makes other team members uncomfortable, may result in corrective action under our progressive discipline process

Workplace Neutrality & Political Activity

Political Neutrality During Work Hours

Domo Cafe is a welcoming environment for all guests and team members, regardless of political beliefs. To protect that environment, the following apply while you are on shift and in uniform:

  • No political buttons, pins, patches, or signage of any kind while in uniform or on the floor
  • No political discussions with guests during service
  • No political campaigning, leafleting, or organizing activities during scheduled work hours
  • Do not post content while on shift that represents or implies Domo Cafe’s position on any political issue

Off-Duty Political Activity

Your Rights — California Labor Code §1101–1102

California law prohibits Domo Cafe from controlling, directing, or influencing your political activities, beliefs, or affiliations outside of work. Your off-duty political activity — including attending protests, rallies, or community events — is your own business and is legally protected.

  • You cannot be terminated, demoted, or penalized for lawful off-duty political activity
  • Your political beliefs and affiliations will never be a factor in hiring, scheduling, or performance reviews
  • Off-duty social media posts about political topics are protected, subject to the standards in our Social Media Policy
  • If protest or political activity causes an absence, standard attendance policy applies — you may use accrued sick leave or take the time unpaid

Immigration Enforcement — ICE Protocol

California AB 450 — Immigrant Worker Protection Act

California law protects employees and employers in the event of an immigration enforcement action. Domo Cafe will fully comply with AB 450 and will not voluntarily cooperate with immigration enforcement beyond what is legally required.

Your rights as an employee:

  • You have the right to remain silent and to decline to answer questions from any government agent
  • You have the right to speak with an attorney before answering questions
  • You may not be retaliated against for exercising these rights

Manager protocol if immigration enforcement agents arrive:

  • Remain calm and professional at all times
  • Ask: “Do you have a judicial warrant signed by a judge?” — an administrative warrant is not sufficient
  • If no judicial warrant is presented: politely decline access to all non-public areas (kitchen, back office, break room, storage)
  • Call the Owner immediately — do not handle this situation alone
  • Do not voluntarily disclose any employee information, records, or schedules without a valid subpoena or judicial order
  • Do not interfere with any employee who wishes to exercise their legal rights
  • Document the date, time, agents’ names, badge numbers, and any documents presented
I-9 Inspection Notice: If Domo Cafe receives a Notice of Inspection for I-9 employment eligibility records, all affected employees and their representatives must be notified within 72 hours. Failure to comply with AB 450 carries penalties of $2,000–$10,000 per violation. We will always notify our team.

Civil Unrest & Safety Closures

If there is active civil unrest, protest activity, or any safety concern near our location, Domo Cafe management reserves the right to close the restaurant for the safety of staff and guests. This is an operational safety decision — not a political one. Affected team members will be notified as early as possible. Reporting time pay rules apply to any shift that is cancelled after staff have been called in (see Part Three).

Service Animal Policy & Training Guide
Purpose

Domo Café is committed to providing equal access to all guests, including those accompanied by service animals, in full compliance with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and California law. All team members are required to read, understand, and follow this policy.

What Is a Service Animal?

Under the ADA, a service animal is a dog (or, in limited cases, a miniature horse) that has been individually trained to perform a specific task or do work for a person with a disability. The animal’s work must be directly related to the person’s disability.

Examples of Qualifying Tasks

  • Guiding a person who is blind
  • Alerting a person who is deaf
  • Detecting and alerting to a seizure before it occurs
  • Alerting to low blood sugar levels
  • Retrieving dropped items or pressing buttons
  • Providing balance or mobility assistance
  • Interrupting PTSD-related episodes
  • Reminding a person to take medication
Not Service Animals Under the ADA: Emotional Support Animals (ESAs), comfort animals, therapy animals, and companion pets do not qualify. The defining requirement is that the animal must be trained to perform a specific task related to a disability — not simply provide emotional comfort by being present.

The Only Two Questions Staff May Ask

If it is not immediately obvious that an animal is a service animal, staff are permitted to ask only these two questions:

Permitted Questions — Memorize These

Question 1: “Is the animal required because of a disability?”

Question 2: “What work or task has the animal been trained to perform?”

Once you receive an acceptable answer — the conversation ends. Do not continue to question the guest.

Questions Staff Are Never Permitted to Ask

The following questions are prohibited under the ADA. Asking them may expose Domo Café to legal liability.
  • “What is your disability?”
  • “Can you show documentation?”
  • “Can you show certification?”
  • “Does the dog have an ID card?”
  • “Can the dog demonstrate the task?”
  • “Why do you need the dog?”

Vests, Certifications & Documentation

Staff must understand and accept the following:

  • Service animals are not required to wear a vest or identifying gear
  • Service animals are not required to carry identification
  • Service animals are not required to be professionally certified
  • Handlers may train their own service animals
  • No paperwork of any kind may be required under the ADA

Where Service Animals Are Permitted

Service animals must be allowed to accompany their handler anywhere guests are normally permitted, including:

  • All dining areas (indoor and outdoor)
  • Waiting areas and ordering lines
  • Outdoor patios open to guests
Do not seat guests with service animals separately or in an isolated area. Do not treat them differently from any other guest.

When a Service Animal May Be Asked to Leave

A service animal may only be asked to leave the premises if one of the following conditions is met:

1. The Animal Is Out of Control

  • Excessive or continuous barking
  • Aggressive behavior toward guests or staff
  • Jumping on customers
  • Running freely without handler control

2. The Animal Is Not Housebroken

  • Urinating indoors
  • Defecating indoors
Required Action on Removal

If a service animal must be removed from the premises, the guest must still be offered service. Suggest that they secure the animal outside and return. Never refuse service to the guest themselves.

Food, Water & Care

Domo Café is not responsible for feeding, watering, walking, or supervising service animals. The handler is solely responsible for the animal’s care at all times.

If a guest requests a plain food item for their service animal (for example, plain cooked chicken without seasoning), treat it as a normal customer request. If Domo Café can reasonably accommodate it, staff may do so.

Manager Quick Reference

Allow Entry If
  • Guest states the animal is required because of a disability
  • AND guest identifies a trained task the animal performs
Do Not Require: Certification • Documentation • Vest • ID card • Demonstration of task
Remove Only If: Animal is out of control (barking, aggression, running freely) OR animal is not housebroken (elimination indoors). Guest must still be offered service.
