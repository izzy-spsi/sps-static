THEY MAY NOT BE SOLD, OFFERED FOR SALE, PLEDGED, HYPOTHECATED, OR OTHERWISE TRANSFERRED EXCEPT PURSUANT TO AN EFFECTIVE REGISTRATION STATEMENT UNDER THE SECURITIES ACT OF 1933, AS AMENDED, OR AN OPINION OF COUNSEL SATISFACTORY TO THE COMPANY THAT REGISTRATION IS NOT REQUIRED UNDER SUCH ACT OR UNLESS SOLD PURSUANT TO RULE 144 UNDER SUCH ACT.
FOR VALUE RECEIVED, Super Power Studios, Inc., a Nevada corporation (the "Company"), hereby promises to pay to the order of Tim Ziss (the "Investor"), the principal sum of Two Hundred Fifty Thousand Dollars (US$250,000.00) (the "Purchase Price"), together with interest thereon from July 1, 2026 (the "Date of Issuance"). Interest shall accrue at the Interest Rate on a simple (non-compounding) basis. No periodic payments of principal or interest shall be required. Unless earlier converted into Conversion Shares pursuant to Section 2, the outstanding principal and all accrued but unpaid interest shall be due and payable in full by the Company on the Maturity Date.

1 Definitions

"Conversion Shares"
(i) With respect to a conversion pursuant to Section 2.1, shares of the Company's Preferred Stock issued in the Next Equity Financing; and
(ii) With respect to a conversion pursuant to Section 2.2 or Section 2.3, shares of the Company's Common Stock.
"Conversion Price"
(i) With respect to Section 2.1, the lower of (A) the product of (1) one (1) minus the Discount and (2) the price per share paid in the Next Equity Financing, or (B) the Valuation Cap divided by the Fully-Diluted Capitalization immediately prior to closing;
(ii) With respect to Section 2.2, the Valuation Cap divided by the Fully-Diluted Capitalization immediately prior to the Corporate Transaction; and
(iii) With respect to Section 2.3, the Valuation Cap divided by the Fully-Diluted Capitalization immediately prior to conversion.
"Corporate Transaction"
The closing of a sale of all or substantially all of the Company's assets; a merger or consolidation resulting in a change of control; or the liquidation, dissolution or winding up of the Company. The sale of shares of Preferred Stock in a bona fide financing shall not be deemed a Corporate Transaction.
"Corporate Transaction Payment"
All accrued and unpaid interest due on this KISS plus one times (1×) the Purchase Price.
"Discount"
Twenty percent (20%).
"Equity Securities"
The Company's Common Stock or Preferred Stock or any securities conferring the right to purchase or convert into Common Stock or Preferred Stock, except securities issued to directors, officers, employees, or consultants as compensation.
"Financial Statements"
An income statement, balance sheet, statement of stockholders' equity, and/or statement of cash flows, as of the end of each fiscal quarter and fiscal year.
"Fully-Diluted Capitalization"
All outstanding Common Stock on a fully-diluted basis, including conversion of all convertible securities, exercise of all options and warrants, and shares reserved under existing stock option plans, excluding the conversion contemplated by the applicable provision of Section 2.
"Holder"
The Investor, for so long as the Investor holds this KISS.
"Interest Rate"
Fifteen percent (15%) per annum, simple interest (non-compounding).
"Maturity Date"
The date that is twenty-four (24) months following the Date of Issuance.
"Next Equity Financing"
The next sale (or series of related sales) by the Company of its Preferred Stock from which the Company receives gross proceeds of not less than $2,500,000 (excluding amounts converted from notes in connection with such sale).
"Participation Amount"
An amount equal to one times (1×) the Purchase Price.
"Valuation Cap"
US$50,000,000.

2 Conversion of the KISS

2.1 Next Equity Financing. Upon the closing of the Next Equity Financing, this KISS will be automatically converted into Conversion Shares equal to the quotient of the Purchase Price plus accrued interest divided by the Conversion Price. Accrued interest may be paid in cash at the Company's option. The Company shall provide at least five (5) days' written notice prior to closing. Conversion Shares shall be issued on the same terms and conditions as Preferred Stock sold in such financing.

2.2 Corporate Transaction. In the event of a Corporate Transaction prior to conversion pursuant to Section 2.1 or 2.3, at the Investor's election: (i) this KISS shall convert into Conversion Shares at the Conversion Price; or (ii) the Investor shall be paid the Corporate Transaction Payment. The Company shall provide at least ten (10) days' written notice prior to closing.

2.3 Maturity Conversion. Unless earlier converted or paid pursuant to Section 2.1 or 2.2, at the election of the Investor at any time on or after the Maturity Date, this KISS shall convert into Conversion Shares equal to the quotient of the Purchase Price plus accrued interest divided by the Conversion Price.

2.4 No Fractional Shares. In lieu of fractional shares, the Company shall pay the Investor cash equal to such fraction multiplied by the Conversion Price.

2.5 Mechanics of Conversion. As promptly as practicable after conversion, the Company shall issue and deliver a certificate or certificates for the Conversion Shares. Conversion may be made contingent upon the closing of the Next Equity Financing or Corporate Transaction.

5.1 Most Favored Nation. If the Company issues convertible instruments with more favorable terms prior to conversion, repayment, or a Corporate Transaction, the Company shall notify the Investor within five (5) days, and the Investor may elect to exchange this KISS for such instrument.

5.2 Major Investor Rights. As the Purchase Price exceeds $150,000, the Investor is a "Major Investor" and is entitled to:

(a) Information Rights. Financial Statements within 30 days after each fiscal quarter-end and 90 days after each fiscal year-end, to the extent prepared.

(b) Participation Rights. Right of first offer to participate in future Equity Security offerings up to the Participation Amount, on the same terms as other investors, with at least ten (10) business days' prior notice.

5.4 Costs and Indemnity. The Company shall pay all reasonable expenses, including attorneys' fees, incurred by the Investor in collecting amounts not paid when due, and shall indemnify and hold the Investor harmless from related losses.

5.5 Security. This KISS is a general unsecured obligation of the Company.

5.6 Successors and Assigns. This KISS binds the respective successors and assigns of the parties. The Company may not assign its obligations without the Investor's prior written consent.

5.7 Governing Law. This KISS shall be governed by and construed under the laws of the State of Nevada, regardless of the laws that might otherwise govern under applicable principles of conflicts of law.

5.8 Notices. All notices shall be in writing and deemed effectively given upon actual receipt, email during business hours, five (5) days by registered mail, or one (1) business day by overnight courier.

5.9 Financing Agreements. The Investor agrees to execute all agreements required in connection with conversion, including purchase, registration, co-sale, and voting rights agreements, on the same terms as Preferred Stock sold in the Next Equity Financing.

5.10 Severability. If any provision is held unenforceable, the balance of this KISS shall remain enforceable in accordance with its terms.

5.11 Acknowledgement. The Investor shall be entitled to the benefit of all adjustments in shares arising from splits, recapitalizations, or similar transactions occurring prior to conversion.

5.12 Further Assurance. The Company shall execute additional documents and provide information as the Investor may reasonably require.

5.13 Transfer. Subject to applicable securities laws, this KISS is transferable in whole or in part upon written notice to the Company.

5.14 Entire Agreement. This KISS constitutes the full and entire understanding between the parties. Amendments require written consent of both parties.

5.15 Exculpation. The Investor is not relying upon any person other than the Company and its officers and directors in making its investment decision.
