03 — Problem

The Industry Is Broken

The $36B taxi industry has been left behind — fragmented, hardware-locked, and shut out of modern ride demand. Mexico is no exception.

01
Taxi drivers can't access Uber demand

Until Flywheel, taxis had zero access to Uber's 12M daily trips — the largest demand channel in the world. Mexico's 150K+ taxis were locked out.

02
Complex hardware locks out drivers

Fleet operators must navigate fragmented meter, dispatch, and tablet systems with no unified solution — especially in emerging markets.

03
Individual drivers locked out entirely

All competitor solutions are fleet-only. Independent licensed taxi drivers — the majority in Mexico — have no path in.

04
No earnings transparency or driver tools

Drivers operate blind — no real-time incentive data, no earnings analytics, no upfront pricing. Driver churn is high.

05
No unified platform OS for taxi operations

Each city runs a different dispatch platform. No operator has built a cross-city taxi OS — until Flywheel.

06
Regional partners have no scalable infrastructure

Local partners like WorkLevel have market access and driver relationships but lack the technology layer to monetize them at scale.
