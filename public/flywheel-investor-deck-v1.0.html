The Problem

The Taxi Industry is
Being Left Behind

600,000+ US professional taxi drivers and millions more globally are operating with broken tools — while Uber and Lyft extract billions in value from their network.

01
Fragmented Infrastructure
Fleets run on 20-year-old dispatch software that can't talk to modern payment rails, mobility apps, or safety systems. No unified data layer.
02
Payments Are a Nightmare
70%+ of taxi revenue still runs through outdated terminal hardware with 3-5% processing costs, slow settlement, and zero driver financial intelligence.
03
No Uber Integration Bridge
Taxis can't access Uber's demand network competitively. The tools to bridge professional fleets to rideshare marketplaces don't exist yet.
04
Zero Driver Intelligence
Drivers have no data: no earnings optimization, no route intelligence, no coaching, no safety scoring. Every trip is a blind decision.
05
Mexico: No Digital Layer
50,000+ taxis in Cancún and CDMX alone operate completely cash-based with zero technology. A massive underserved market with no incumbent.
06
Operator Economics Are Broken
Fleet operators have no visibility into vehicle performance, utilization, or revenue optimization. They're flying blind while losing market share to TNCs.
⚡
The Market Signal

NYC alone has 80,000+ taxi medallion vehicles. San Diego has 1,200+. Mexico City has 100,000+ registered taxis. These fleets desperately need a modern OS. Nobody has built it yet — until Flywheel.

Transportation OS

One platform. Dispatch, payments, AI intelligence, Uber integration, and fleet analytics — all unified under a single flywheel that compounds with every driver and every trip.
