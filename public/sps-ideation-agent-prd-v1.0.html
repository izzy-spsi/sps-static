Vision
Start with the founder.
Not the idea.
"That is exactly what I was trying to build,
but I couldn't explain it."
— What every founder should say after the session

Most incubators start with the idea. SPS starts with the founder. This agent's primary job is not to create ideas — it's to discover who the founder is, what problem they are uniquely qualified to solve, and whether there is genuine founder-market fit.

The agent should feel like part Y-Combinator partner, part executive coach, part venture capitalist, part therapist, part co-founder. The output isn't an idea — it's a founder-centered venture thesis that becomes the input to every subsequent SPS agent.

Requirements · Agent Inputs
What the founder provides
REQ-001
Required Fields — Identity
Agent must collect Name, Email, LinkedIn URL, Current Role, Industry, and Location before beginning the interview.
Proposed
REQ-002
Optional Uploads — Documents
Agent must accept optional uploads: Resume, Website URL, existing Pitch Deck, or Concept Document. These inform the agent's context before the interview begins.
Proposed
REQ-003
Context Ingestion
When optional documents are provided, the agent must read and reference them during the interview — avoiding redundant questions already answered in the materials.
Proposed
REQ-004
Session Persistence
Interview state must be saved continuously. If the founder disconnects, they must be able to resume from the last completed phase without losing prior responses.
Proposed
REQ-005
Founder Identity Verification
Email must be verified before session begins. LinkedIn URL must be valid format. No anonymous sessions allowed.
Proposed
Requirements · Interview Structure
7 phases. One founder.
REQ-006
Phase 1 — Founder Discovery
Goal: Understand who this person is
▼
Tell me about yourself.
What do you do today?
What are you exceptionally good at?
What are people constantly asking for your help with?
What industries have you worked in?
What have you built before?
Have you started a company before?
What accomplishments are you most proud of?
What frustrates you about your current work?
REQ-007
Phase 2 — The Personal Story
Goal: Find emotional connection
▼
What problem keeps bothering you?
When did you first notice it?
Why does this matter to you?
Have you personally experienced it?
Can you tell me a story?
Who else experiences this?
What would happen if this problem never gets solved?
Why are you the person to solve it?
REQ-008
Phase 3 — The Problem
Goal: Discover the actual pain
▼
Describe the problem in one sentence.
Who has this problem?
How often does it happen?
How painful is it?
How much money does it cost?
How much time does it waste?
How are people solving it today?
What frustrates you about current solutions?
Why haven't existing solutions solved it?
REQ-009
Phase 4 — The Customer
Goal: Identify ICP
▼
Who specifically experiences this? (Consumers / Businesses / Government / Non-profits)
How old are they? Income level?
Industry? Title? Location?
What triggers the pain?
What are they currently paying to solve it?
REQ-010
Phase 5 — The Solution
Goal: Articulate the vision
▼
Describe your solution.
What does the customer do first?
What happens next?
What makes this different?
Why is this better?
How will people discover it?
How do you make money?
REQ-011
Phase 6 — Founder Commitment
Goal: Determine seriousness
▼
How many hours per week can you dedicate?
How much money are you willing to invest?
Have you quit a job before?
Would you work on this for 3 years? 5 years?
What happens if this fails?
Why won't you quit?
REQ-012
Phase 7 — Founder Superpower Discovery ⭐
Goal: Signature SPS feature — uncover the unfair advantage
▼
What comes naturally to you?
What do people consistently compliment you on?
When do you feel most energized?
What work drains you?
What could you talk about for hours?
What have you done that others find difficult?
What unfair advantage do you have?
What is your superpower?
Requirements · Adaptive Engine
Never static. Always drilling.
REQ-013
Dynamic Follow-Up Questions
The agent must generate contextual follow-up questions based on each answer. It must never proceed to the next phase while clarity is still missing from the current one.
Proposed
REQ-014
Assumption Challenging
When a founder states an assumption (e.g., "there are no competitors"), the agent must challenge it with evidence-seeking questions before proceeding.
Proposed
REQ-015
Story & Evidence Seeking
For every claim, the agent must seek a concrete story or evidence ("Can you tell me a story about when this happened?"). Abstract answers are not accepted.
Proposed
REQ-016
Drill-Down Until Clarity
The agent must continue probing a topic until a specific, credible, and testable answer emerges. Depth of exploration is more important than speed of completion.
Proposed
Requirements · Guardrails
Vague answers are rejected.
REQ-017
Vague Answer Detection
The agent must detect and reject vague responses including: "I want to help people", "Everyone is my customer", "There are no competitors", and similar non-specific answers. It must redirect with specificity prompts.
Proposed
REQ-018
Specificity Enforcement
When a vague answer is detected, the agent must respond with: "Can you be more specific?", "Can you give an example?", "Tell me a story.", "Who exactly?", or "Why exactly?" before moving forward.
Proposed
REQ-019
Phase Gate — Minimum Clarity Threshold
Each phase has a minimum clarity threshold. The agent must not advance to the next phase until the threshold is met. Thresholds are defined per phase in the scoring model.
Proposed
Requirements · Internal Scoring Model
Four scores. One verdict.
REQ-020
Founder-Market Fit Score
0 – 100
Experience in the domain
Passion for the problem
Credibility with the customer
Relevant network
Domain expertise
Personal connection to pain
REQ-021
Problem Strength Score
0 – 100
Frequency of occurrence
Urgency to solve
Severity of pain
Economic cost of problem
Dissatisfaction with current solutions
REQ-022
Execution Potential Score
0 – 100
Time commitment available
Financial resources
Relevant skill set
Network to leverage
Demonstrated determination
REQ-023
Overall SPS Score
0 – 100 · Composite of REQ-020, 021, 022
Weighted average across all three scores
Drives the final Green/Yellow/Red recommendation
Visible to founder in their Venture Brief
Requirements · Final Deliverable
The Founder Venture Brief

A professional, structured document generated at the end of every session. This becomes the input to every downstream SPS agent.

REQ-024 · Section 1
Executive Summary
One-page overview of the founder, problem, and opportunity.
REQ-025 · Section 2
Founder Profile
Background, experience, strengths, and superpowers.
REQ-026 · Section 3
Founder Story
Personal narrative — why this founder, why this problem, why now.
REQ-027 · Section 4
Problem Statement
Detailed articulation of the pain being solved.
REQ-028 · Section 5
Customer Profile
ICP definition, pain points, and jobs to be done.
REQ-029 · Section 6
Proposed Solution
Vision, features, and differentiation.
REQ-030 · Section 7
Founder-Market Fit Analysis
Strengths, weaknesses, risks, and unfair advantages.
REQ-031 · Section 8
Key Assumptions
What must be true for this venture to succeed.
REQ-032 · Section 9
Open Questions
Unknowns requiring validation before proceeding.
REQ-033 · Section 10
SPS Recommendation
Green / Yellow / Red light with rationale and next steps.
Requirements · SPS Recommendation
One of three outcomes.
🟢
REQ-034 · Green Light
Proceed to Market Research Agent. Founder-Market Fit is strong. Problem is real. Commitment is credible.
🟡
REQ-035 · Yellow Light
Refine the idea. One or more dimensions need strengthening before moving forward. Agent provides specific guidance.
🔴
REQ-036 · Red Light
Reconsider the opportunity. Fundamental misalignment detected. Agent explains why and suggests alternative paths.
